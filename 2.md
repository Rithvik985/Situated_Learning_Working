•	Ensures access control and oversees all user and data management at the global level.

•	Has access to reports and data metadata.

3. Student Workflow
a. Course and Context Selection
•	The student logs into the platform and selects:
o	Course from the available course list.
o	Relevant handouts for reference.
o	Contextual inputs through dropdown menus such as:
	Domain: e.g., Manufacturing, IT, Healthcare, etc.
	Service or Product Category: e.g., DevOps, ERP, Cloud, etc.
	Department or Application Area: where the chosen topic will be implemented.
b. Question Generation
•	Based on the contextual selections, the system automatically generates 5–6 moderate-difficulty, unique problem statements.
•	These questions are tailored for each student AI-based question generation logic.
c. Question Selection and Faculty Approval
•	The student selects one problem statement from the list.
•	The chosen problem statement is forwarded to the respective faculty for approval.
•	Once approved, the student can proceed to work on the assignment.
d. Assignment Submission
•	The student prepares the report and uploads it to the platform.
•	Upon submission, the AI engine performs the initial check using a standard rubric-based assessment model.
e. AI check and Feedback
The AI performs multiple checks and generates constructive feedback:
1.	Originality Check – Verifies that the content is not AI-generated, maintaining a defined uniqueness threshold.
2.	Feedback Report – Generates a short key pointer to improve the report, highlighting:
o	Key strengths
o	Areas requiring improvement
f. Feedback Iteration
•	The student can review the AI feedback and either:
o	Accept the evaluation if satisfied, or
o	Revise and re-upload the report for re-evaluation.
•	The loop continues until the student accepts the AI feedback report.
________________________________________
4. Faculty Workflow
Once the student has approved the AI-generated suggestions, it transitions to the Faculty Dashboard.
a. Faculty Review Process
•	Faculty applies the standard rubric to the student’s final submission.
•	The system automatically generates:
1.	A Summarized Report – providing key highlights and score overview.
2.	A Detailed Report – including rubric-wise ratings and observations.
b. Faculty Decision
•	Faculty reviews the AI feedback and has the option to:
o	Accept the AI Feedback:
	Validate the AI evaluation.
	Assign final marks directly within the portal.
o	Reject or Modify AI Feedback:
	Provide additional remarks and score to train the AI model.
c. Assignment Closure
•	Upon finalization, the faculty updates the marks and remarks in the system, officially closing the assignment.
